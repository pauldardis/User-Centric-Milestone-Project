Mallorica

Ride the longest amateur sportive in Europe and experience the magical atmosphere of the Mallorca 312

Our 7 day cycling holiday in Mallorca offers a wonderful mix of rolling routes and stunning days in the Mountains. The days can be flexible to suit the group but we make sure you get to see all the iconic climbs including the world famous Sa Calobra and Formentor. The smooth tarmac and quiet roads make for the ideal cycling holiday in Mallorca. With many pro teams, clubs and amateur riders flocking to the island each year, it really is a must for any cyclist.

Itinerary

Arrive into Palma where you will be met by our tour leader before transferring to our hotel in Puerto de Pollensa, where we build the bikes and receive our welcome briefing

Slightly easier day with an out and back to the lighthouse on some of the smoothest tarmac around stop for coffee at the lighthouse and roll back just ride at your own pace spinning the legs

The Mallorca 312 is one of the toughest endurance events in the sportive calendar. The long course takes in 312 kilometres of riding with over 5,000m of climbing. There are 2 shorter options available Mallorica 225 (4000m of elevation) and Mallorca 167 (2500m of elevation)

Today we relax and either rest by the pool or have a leisurely cycle to the local towns

We head out today on small lanes passing through Selva and Lioseta our first climb of the day is just after Bunyola we then drop down into Soller and catch the ferry to Sa Calobra where we start the most famous climb on the island because it’s the most glorious spaghetti mess of a road, looping up and over itself for 9.4km.

Short day with just 1 climb , we head out along the coast to Alcudia and then cut inland to Sleva. From here we start the climb up to Lluc followed by the long descent back Pollensa where we pack our bags and bikes and chill out with a few beers before transferring to Plama airport for a return flights home.
